Research

Downstream impacts of mines on agriculture in Africa

Vashold, Pirich, Heinze & Kuschnig (2026), Journal of Development Economics, 179, 103671.

Abstract. Mining operations in Africa are expanding rapidly, creating negative externalities that remain poorly understood. We provide causal evidence for the impact of water pollution from mines on downstream vegetation and agriculture across the continent. We exploit a natural experiment, where mines cause a discontinuity in water pollution along river networks, to compare vegetation health in upstream and downstream locations. We find that mines significantly reduce peak vegetation downstream by 1.3–1.5%, impairing the productivity of over 74,000 km² of croplands — annual losses of 91,000–205,000 tons of cereal crops in the immediate vicinity alone, with particularly severe effects in fertile regions and gold-mining areas. The findings highlight substantial externalities of mining and an urgent need for oversight and regulation.
