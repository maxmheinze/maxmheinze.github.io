Research

Downstream impacts of mines on agriculture in Africa

Vashold, Pirich, Heinze & Kuschnig (2026), Journal of Development Economics, 179, 103671.

Abstract. Mining operations in Africa are expanding rapidly, creating negative externalities that remain poorly understood. We provide causal evidence for the impact of water pollution from mines on downstream vegetation and agriculture. We exploit a natural experiment, where mines cause a discontinuity in water pollution along river networks, to compare vegetation health upstream and downstream. Mines significantly reduce peak vegetation downstream by 1.3–1.5%, impairing over 74,000 km² of croplands.
